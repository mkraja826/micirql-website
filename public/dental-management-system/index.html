Overview

Managing a dental clinic needs more than paper files

Managing a dental clinic is not only about treating patients. Every day, a clinic has to handle patient records, OP fees, appointments, prescriptions, X-rays, pending payments, follow-ups, staff work, and owner reports. When all this is handled through paper files or scattered WhatsApp messages, it becomes difficult to track everything properly.

DMS Clinic is designed especially for dental clinics where receptionists, doctors, and clinic owners work together. The goal is not to make clinic work complicated with too many unnecessary features. The goal is to create a clean digital workflow that saves time and keeps patient records easy to find.

Why clinics need it

Common problems in paper-based dental clinics

Many dental clinics still depend on paper records. This works in the beginning, but as patient visits increase, problems start appearing.

  • Old patient files are hard to find
  • Pending payments are forgotten
  • Follow-up patients are missed
  • Prescription photos are not organized
  • X-rays are stored in different places
  • Owner cannot easily check daily revenue
  • Reception and doctor workflow becomes confusing
  • Daily patient reports become inaccurate

DMS Clinic is built to solve these daily clinic problems with a simple, role-based workflow.

Workflow

How DMS Clinic works

When a patient comes to the clinic, the receptionist adds the patient details and collects the OP fee. The patient then appears in the waiting queue for the doctor.

The doctor opens the patient profile, adds the visit details, uploads prescription or X-ray files, and schedules the next appointment if needed.

The owner can later check patient count, revenue, pending payments, and clinic activity.

Simple flow: Reception → Patient check-in → Doctor visit → Prescription/X-ray upload → Payment → Follow-up.

Key Features

Features built for dental clinic operations

1. Patient Management

DMS Clinic stores patient details like name, phone number, age, gender, address, and medical history. Old patients can be added and managed properly. When an old patient visits again, the system can count them in that day’s patient list based on their visit.

2. Appointment and Waiting Queue

Reception staff can check in patients and send them to the doctor queue. Doctors can easily see who is waiting. Once the doctor adds a visit, the patient is marked as completed and removed from the waiting queue.

3. OP Fee and Payment Tracking

Track OP fees, treatment payments, medication fees, X-ray fees, and pending payments. Clinic owners can view today’s revenue, pending amount, OP fee collection, treatment collection, and pending payment collection.

4. Prescription and X-ray Uploads

Doctors or clinic staff can upload prescription photos, X-rays, reports, and before/after photos. Visual dental records stay connected to the correct patient profile instead of being scattered in phone galleries.

5. Follow-up Reminders

Dental treatments often need follow-up visits. DMS Clinic helps track follow-up dates for root canals, braces, extractions, implants, crowns, and review visits.

6. Role-Based Access

DMS Clinic supports Owner / Head Doctor, Working Doctor, and Receptionist roles. Reception handles patient entry and payments, doctors handle visits and clinical records, and owners review activity and revenue.
